Guidelines for Speakers

Considering the widespread interest in Quantum Information Science, it is important to keep in mind that the expected audience does not have a specific scientific profile. People coming from at least 4 different departments have shown a high degree of interest: Physics, Electrical and Computer Engineering, Math and Material Science. Moreover, the expected background of the audience can range from undergraduates to full professors. As a result of this, the speakers are highly encouraged to:
  • Tailor their presentations to a general - but still scientific - audience.
  • Use a generic (as opposed to field-specific) language, to lower the barrier of understanding and avoid the implicit assumptions due to the field-specific background we all have.
  • Keep in mind that we might have students and professors with a low degree of experience and understanding of quantum mechanics and its perks. As such, the speakers should be open and encourage questions from the audience.
  • Coordinate with the speaker they have been paired-up with to discuss a common strategy. This aims at providing a coherent and cohesive picture of the chosen topic, both from the experimental and theoretical perspective.
